Tucker Blakely, Knox County Deputy Officer, Dies after Shooting

September 08, 2024
post

Tucker Blakely, a dedicated 29-year-old Deputy Officer of Knox County, tragically lost his life at the University of Tennessee Medical Center. He was shot while bravely carrying out his duties in the line of duty. The distressing news was shared by Knox County Sheriff Tom Spangler during a Monday night announcement. To gain a deeper understanding of this heartbreaking event, continue reading this article.

Tucker Blakely Died: How Did It Happen?

According to the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation, the incident began when deputies were called to a domestic dispute in the 3200 block of Denver Lane at around 11:15 p.m. on Sunday. The Knox County dispatch received a call reporting the domestic situation, prompting deputies from the Knox County Sheriff’s Office to respond. Upon arriving at the scene, they encountered an armed man at the residence. The situation quickly escalated, leading to an exchange of gunfire between the individual and at least one deputy. Tragically, the man, later identified as Matthew Logan Rose, was struck and pronounced dead at the scene. One of the deputies also sustained serious injuries from the gunfire and was rushed to a nearby hospital for medical attention.

The investigation into this tragic incident will be led by the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation, as requested by District Attorney General Charme Allen.

Deputy Tucker Blakely, who was injured in the line of duty, was transported to the University of Tennessee Medical Center, where he valiantly fought for his life but was, unfortunately, pronounced dead on Monday night. His fellow officers solemnly escorted his body to the Medical Examiner’s Office.

On Monday morning, Sheriff Tom Spangler requested prayers for his wounded deputy. Various social media posts expressing solidarity and well-wishes for the officer circulated, with support pouring in from the Tennessee Highway Patrol, Knox County Mayor Glenn Jacob, and Chief Paul Noel of the Knoxville Police Department.
