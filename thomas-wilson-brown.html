    Thomas Wilson Brown Career

    Brown got his first position as the son of a cattle rancher and rodeo queen by responding to a tiny advertisement in a local newspaper in Santa Fe, New Mexico, searching for a young kid who could ride a horse.

    After completing Silverado, Brown worked along with JoBeth Williams, Lindsay Wagner, Jim Caviezel, and Bruce Dern in a number of movies during his adolescent years. He is known for his role as “Jason Lochner” in the television drama Knots Landing, his notable role as “Little Russ Thompson” in Honey, I Shrunk the Kids, and his co-starring role with Winona Ryder in Welcome Home, Roxy Carmichael.

    Brown appeared as the rebellious protagonist Joe on Beverly Hills, 90210 (episode “The Game is Chicken”) in 1993. He also had some TV shows such as CSI, Daybreak, Nash Bridges, and Walker, Texas Ranger. He also played a small role in the 2007 film Urban Justice by Steven Seagal. Our House (1986 TV series) includes smaller roles.

    Brown co-starred in 2010 in The Mooring’s autonomous thriller. In 2017, Brown co-starred in Last Three Days with Robert Palmer Watkins

    He is still performing in films, on stage, and on television as of 2010, but has extended his position in the film industry to include writer, director, and producer. He is also a co-managing member and co-owner of Thunder Basin Pictures, an autonomous movie production company.
