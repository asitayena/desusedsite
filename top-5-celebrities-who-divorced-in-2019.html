The fans across the world witnessed their favorite celebrity getting married in 2019. But they were also a spectator of their ideal celebs divorce settlements. Divorces are rough, and with the media continually lurking above the celebs, make it harder.

From Jeff Bezos to Nicholas Cage, the celebrities paid some big money during the settlements. In this article, we are going to countdown the top 5 celebrity divorce in 2019.

Nicholas Cage and Erika Koike

Ghost Rider star Nicholas Cage marriage with Erika Koike just lived for four days. The Hollywood actor tied the knot with Make-up artist Koike on March 23, 2019, in Las Vegas.

According to TMZ, the couple fought hours after the wedding. In just four days, Cage filed for an annulment from Erika. Koike, on the other hand, agreed for separation but wants his money.

The former duo was dating since April 2018. As per Cage, the actor said, he reacted on impulse and did not understand the full impact of his actions. Koike also wants Nicholas to pay for her legal fees. 

As per USA Today, the pair drank to the point of intoxication at their matrimony and argued on Erika's relationship with another person. On May 1, 2019, the court granted them the status of a divorcee.

Colin Firth and Livia 

Even when two people are together for a long time, they might not know the other person completely. The rough patch between the couple created distance between them, leading to divorce. Colin Firth, after 22-years of marriage, splits from his wife, Livia Giuggiolo.

According to Hello! the duo has separated but still maintains a perfect friendship. The representatives of the pair refused to comment on the split. Hello! says that as per some sources, Livia started having an affair with a childhood friend some two years back. 

The reports suggest that Livia fell in love with her childhood friend, Marco Branaccia. But the duo never commentated on anything back then until 2018 when the pair filed a complained against Marco. As per the complaint, Marco was stalking Livia, but Marco denied all the charges.

He further said they were romantically in love, and Livia was ready to leave Colin for him. Well, as of now, Livia goes as the former wife of Colin, but the duo remains close for their two children.

Larry King and Shawn Southwick King

The 85-year-old Larry King got married for the seventh time in September 1997. They are also parents to two adult sons, Chance King and Cannon King. According to The Blast, Larry filed for the split in Los Angeles Superior Court.

As per the reports, the listing date of the separation is June 6, 2019. The couple says irreconcilable differences as the reason for ending their relationship. This is the second time they tried to end their marital status. 

In 2010, the pair filed for separation, but they ultimately reconciled. Not just that in 2016, their marital relationship took the hit after the rumors of Shawn's infidelity. It was just the rumor as Larry also dismissed it in an interview. The ending of the relationship with Shawn is Larry's seventh divorce.

Zooey Deschanel and Jacob Pechenik

The New Girl actress Zooey Deschanel and her husband of four years released a joint statement announcing the end of their relationship. 

They said,

"After much discussion and a long period of Contemplation we have decided we are better off as friends, business partners and co-parents rather than life partners".

The couple raises two children Elsie Pechenik and Charlie Pechenik. After May 2018, the pair were not spotted by the media; however, Zooey and Jacob Pechenik announced their split on September 6, 2019. 

Jeff Bezos and Mackenzie Bezos

The most significant divorce settlement of all time where Jeff paid $38 billion. Amazon founder Jeff Bezos ended his relationship with his wife Mackenzie Bezos on April 4, 2019, through a official statement.

The couple married in 1993 a year before Jeff started Amazon from his garage. Not just billion-dollar, she also receives a 4% stake in the Trillion dollar company. As per The Guardian, Mackenzie signed the Giving Pledge created by Warren Buffett and Bill Gates for the charity.
