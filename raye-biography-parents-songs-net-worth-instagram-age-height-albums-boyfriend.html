Early Life & Education

Raye, born on October 24, 1997, in Tooting, London, England, is a 26-year-old British singer. Her heritage is a blend of Ghanaian-Swiss from her mother and English from her father, who hails from Yorkshire. Growing up, she was immersed in music, thanks to her father’s guitar skills.

Raye‘s musical journey began early as she attended the prestigious BRIT School for Performing Arts and Technology, where she fine-tuned her talents. She identifies as mixed-race, embracing her Ghanaian and English roots. Her academic path included Woodcote High School.

The BRIT School was instrumental in her development, providing valuable exposure and experience. After graduating, Raye entered the music scene in 2014. Known for her potent vocals and infectious pop melodies, she’s collaborated with industry giants.

Career

In December 2014, at just 17, Raye released her debut EP, Welcome to the Winter, marking her entry into the music scene. Subsequent singles, Flowers and Alien, in 2015 and 2016, gained her recognition and popularity.

Raye‘s career took flight in 2015 when she featured on Clean Bandit‘s By Your Side, a UK chart hit. This led to a record deal with Polydor Records and the release of her debut single, “I, U, Us,” in 2016, earning critical acclaim.

Collaborations with artists like Jonas Blue, David Guetta, and Charli XCX further solidified her position. She also garnered nominations for prestigious awards such as the BRIT Awards.

Raye‘s multifaceted talent and innovative music style have made her a rising star in the industry, earning her a spot on Forbes’ 30 Under 30 list in 2018. Despite her success, she remains dedicated to pushing the boundaries of her craft, promising an exciting future in music.
