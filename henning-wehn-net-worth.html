FAQs about Henning Wehn

Who is Henning Wehn?

Henning Wehn is a German stand-up comedian known as the German Comedy Ambassador in London. He’s famous for his appearances on British panel shows and his unique take on stereotypes about Germans.

When and where was he born?

He was born on April 10, 1974, in Hagen, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany.

How did he start his comedy career?

Initially working in customer relations in Germany and then in the marketing department of Wycombe Wanderers in the UK, he ventured into stand-up comedy after watching an open mic night in 2002, deciding he could do just as well.

What are some of Henning Wehn’s notable appearances?

Wehn has appeared on several British panel shows, including 8 Out of 10 Cats, Have I Got News for You, Room 101, QI, and Would I Lie to You?.

Does he have any special television projects?

Yes, among his projects are The Road to Rio, a travelogue in Brazil exploring the country’s sporting culture, and An Immigrant’s Guide to Britain, a Channel 4 program mixing light-hearted and serious topics.
